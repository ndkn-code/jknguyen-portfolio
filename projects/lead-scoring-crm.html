How It Works

n8n workflow: Form webhook → Lead scoring → Jira ticket creation → Google Sheets logging

Website form submissions trigger automated scoring based on 5 factors. Qualified leads create prioritized Jira tickets with all contact info, while data syncs to Google Sheets for analytics.

Key Features

calculate

Multi-Factor Scoring Algorithm

Scores leads on source (+45 for referrals), urgency (test date timing), course type, profile (age/country), and intent signals

low_priority

Priority-Based Routing

Score ≥100 = Highest, ≥85 = High, ≥65 = Medium, ≥45 = Low. Jira tickets auto-prioritized for sales team

restore

Partial Lead Recovery

Abandoned forms with 50+ gatekeeper score still create tickets. Recovers leads others would miss

timeline

Sales Funnel Tracking

Status flow: Queue → Contacted → Assigned → Won/Lost/MIA. Every transition synced to Google Sheets

warning_amber

Late Penalty System

Daily automation checks overdue tickets. Escalating penalties: -1, -2, -3, then -117 for chronic delays

layers

Dual Data Layer

Jira for workflow/accountability, Google Sheets for analytics/reporting. Bi-directional sync via webhooks

Why These 5 Scoring Factors

lightbulb

Developed through interviews, not guesswork. I talked to the CEO (5+ years SAT teaching), other tutors, sales team, and alumni students to understand what makes a lead convert.

person_add Source (+45 for referrals)

Sales confirmed referred leads closed at much higher rates, making them worth prioritizing.

schedule Urgency (test date timing)

3-6 months out is the sweet spot. Enough time to improve, urgent enough to commit.

school Course type

Pre-SAT and 1-1 students showed higher intent and lifetime value.

person Profile (age/country)

US-based students aged 16-18 matched our ideal customer profile from alumni interviews.

Why the Penalty System

lightbulb

First few weeks after launch, sales team wasn't adhering to follow-up timelines. Rather than micromanage from across the world, I built automated accountability: system tracks overdue tickets and escalates penalties daily. Penalties are visible to everyone, creating peer accountability without requiring me to chase people.

Daily Jira automation runs at 2 PM EST checking for overdue tickets in Queue status:
